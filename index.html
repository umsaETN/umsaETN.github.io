Discover our history

The "Universidad Mayor de San Andrés (UMSA)", was created in 1830 during the government of the Marshal Andrés de Santa Cruz, as Universidad de Nuestra Señora de La Paz. On May 28, 1927, the University takes its current name of Universidad Mayor de San Andrés, acquiring its status as "Autonomous University" in 1930. The Electronics Engineering career integrates the offer of degree formation of the Universidad Mayor of San Andrés. This public institution of higher education, founded in 1971, constitutes an internationally recognized university that has historically developed teaching, research and extension activities in practically all branches of knowledge. The Faculty of Engineering is the academic service responsible for the conception, implementation and evaluation of the career. The first activities related to Electronics Engineering consisted in the introduction of electrotechnics in the Engineering courses in 1976 and the creation of the Electronics Applied Institute in 1996. In 1997 the Electronics Engineering Career Study Plan was approved, which included the specializations of control, computer systems, and telecommunications. Has successfully achieved accreditation at the Cacei Mexico level.
